Part I — The Final Patch of Dawntrail

Patch 7.5 — Trail to the Heavens

Yoshida calls 7.5 a "last major patch before we enter a new phase." It is the most loaded transitional patch the team has ever shipped: a Beastmaster reveal, a Kefka Ultimate, the conclusion of the Echoes of Vana'diel saga, sweeping system reworks to dyes and the Armoire, and an MSQ split across two parts that quietly walks the Warrior of Light to the threshold of the Fourth.

Main Scenario

The Warrior returns from the Ninth — and the key calls again

The MSQ in 7.5 is split into two parts. Part I drops April 28; Part II ("7.56") arrives in early September. Yoshida confirmed both halves carry "key components" of the storyline, with the second half deliberately delayed to better bridge into the Godless Realms Saga.

Expect this patch to read as setup. The mystery of the key, an unsettling message from an old ally, and the continued unraveling of why the Ninth went the way it did all converge here. The thread leads, eventually, somewhere very cold.

Trial

A new trial in two difficulties

A new normal/extreme trial accompanies Part I of the MSQ. Mount drops follow the usual extreme cadence. The 7.5 EX mount was teased at the Live Letter alongside several others — golden winged, mechanized hand, platypus, and a new motorcycle.

New Limited Job

Beastmaster — solo, axe-swinging, monster-tamer

A melee DPS limited job wielding a one-handed axe. Starts at level 1, caps at 50. Up to 50 different beasts can be tamed and called to your side, with up to three summoned at once. Beasts you've captured walk beside you in the world.

Unlock requires any DoW/DoM at 50+, A Realm Reborn complete, and Dawntrail registered. Crucially: Yoshi-P confirmed it stays solo-friendly. No raid lockouts.

Alliance Raid

Echoes of Vana'diel — Windurst: The Third Walk

The closing chapter of the FFXI crossover series. The early footage Yoshida walked through at the Live Letter ended at the first boss: a very large Shantotto. The duty visits Windurst proper for the first time, and the soundtrack continues the FFXI arrangement work that has defined the series.

Ultimate Raid — coming in 7.51

Dancing Mad (Ultimate) — Kefka takes the stage

Square Enix made it official at the keynote: Dancing Mad (Ultimate) launches with Patch 7.51 on June 2, 2026. The next jewel in the Ultimate crown after FRU. Mechanically, expect the four-tier transformation conceit from the original FFVI fight to be the dramatic spine of the encounter — though the team is keeping specifics close.

This is also one of two Ultimates announced for the year — a second new Ultimate is already confirmed for Evercold's patch cycle.

Field Operations

Occult Crescent: North Horn (7.55)

The next chapter of Dawntrail's field operations content launches July 28 with Patch 7.55. New zone, new monsters, new Critical Encounters, increased knowledge level cap, and several new Phantom Jobs to add to the roster. Phantom Weapons get a new round of enhancements with new particle effects.

Cosmic Exploration

Auxesia — a new star (7.51)

The next planet in Cosmic Exploration arrives June 2 with Patch 7.51. New gear, new gathering, new crafting goals tied to its restoration. Pairs cleanly with the dye consolidation if you've been deep in cosmetic crafting.

System reworks: Armoire & Dye

For the first time ever, the Armoire is being expanded to hold dungeon gear. At 7.5 launch this means every gear set from every 7.x dungeon — Ihuykatumu, Origenics, Alexandria, Strayborough Deadwalk, Yuweyawata, Underkeep, Mistwake, and the new 7.5 dungeon. All AF (job-specific) armor is also covered.

Yoshi-P framed this as the start of a long campaign: future patches will gradually backfill older expansions' dungeon gear and introduce outfit glamours for them. The "system has been built" — the rest is just rolling it out.

Alongside it, the dye system is consolidating roughly a hundred individual dyes into three category bundles (Type A: 85 ARR-era dyes; Type B: 9 Ishgardian Restoration dyes; Type C: 11 Cosmic Exploration dyes). A one-button Calamity Salvager exchange will swap your old stock to the new format. Cash-shop dyes are unaffected.

The Cold Comes Calling
Part II — The Sixth Expansion

Evercold

The Godless Realms Saga begins. January 2027.

Announced at FINAL FANTASY XIV Fan Festival 2026 in Anaheim, Evercold opens an entirely new story arc — the first true reset of the game's narrative cosmology since A Realm Reborn. The Warrior of Light travels to the Fourth, a reflection of Hydaelyn that has fallen to ruin and is slowly being consumed by ice. Yoshi-P framed it as not just a new expansion, but a new FFXIV: a deliberate evolution of how the game is built, paced, and played.

The setting: The Fourth

A reflection of Hydaelyn that has fallen into ruin and is being slowly devoured by an ever-spreading ice. Concept art shown at Fanfest depicted a marked artistic departure from anything in the game so far — bleak, monumental, painterly.

Yoshida did not name the explorable zones at Fanfest, citing the ongoing 7.5x storyline. He did, however, show a "massive new danger" referred to as an Arcane Giant, and a first look at a new feline-coded NPC race that bears some resemblance to Monster Hunter's Palicoes.

The Story Arc

The Godless Realms Saga

The first new story saga in FFXIV's history. Hydaelyn–Zodiark — A Realm Reborn through Endwalker — was the first. Dawntrail was a coda. Evercold opens chapter one of the second. Yoshi-P framed this as the most consequential narrative reset in the game's life and confirmed that the patch cycle from 7.56 onward exists to walk players cleanly to its threshold.

8-Player Raid

Theme reserved for Berlin

The new 8-player raid series exists, but its theme will be revealed at the European Fan Festival. Notable structural change: alongside Normal and Savage, Evercold introduces a third difficulty tier sitting between them. Ultimate raids are unaffected and continue on their own track — a second new Ultimate is also confirmed for the 8.x cycle.

Alliance Raid

Evangelion — Ghosts of Desire

An alliance raid crossover with Neon Genesis Evangelion, developed by Square Enix in collaboration with Khara, Inc. for the anime's 30th anniversary. Following the structural footprint of YoRHA: Dark Apocalypse — three 24-player raids spread across the patch cycle, each tied to a major story chapter.

The "new FFXIV"

More than any expansion since Heavensward, Evercold is being framed as a structural reset. A list of changes that, individually, would be major; collectively, they amount to a deliberate rewrite of how the game is paced and progressed.

Job Design

Reborn Mode & Evolved Mode

The 21 non-limited jobs split into two playable variants. Reborn Mode is the game as it currently exists — your rotation, leveled and adjusted to 110. Evolved Mode is "individuality-emphasizing" — fewer inputs, distinct playstyles per job, designed to differentiate jobs that have converged in feel.

New jobs introduced in 8.0 onward will only have an Evolved Mode.

Two New Jobs

Tank + Ranged DPS

Following the pattern Shadowbringers set with Gunbreaker and Dancer. Confirmed roles only — specifics held for European Fanfest in Berlin. Yoshi-P took the stage in a "Guess Who's Back" shirt, fueling speculation that one or both will be a returning Final Fantasy classic rather than wholly new.

Armoury System

Item level follows the player, not the job

For the first time, gear in your Armoury reflects your highest item level across all jobs, irrespective of the job equipped. This means you can take an off-job into Savage prog without re-gearing it from scratch — a fundamental shift in how alt jobs are maintained at endgame.

Progression Cadence

Weekly, not daily

A new Adventurer Activity UI replaces the daily-grind structure. Unearned tomestones from previous weekly cycles can be earned retroactively. The pivot is explicit: Yoshida acknowledged "fight fatigue" and is structurally reducing the cost of taking a break from the game.

Patch Cadence

Seasons (without the battle pass)

An even-numbered patch and its odd-numbered follow-up will now be referred to as one Season (e.g., 8.0 + 8.1 = Season 1). The team is using the language to bundle thinking around content; no price changes, no microtransactions, no battle pass. The audible groan in the keynote audience reportedly turned to cheers.

Matchmaking

Same-region matching at last

Players from any World in the same region — including Party Finder — can finally match together. Tested first in Japan during 7.5x, then expanded to NA, EU, and China.

Duty Support

Duo Duty Support

Duty Support gets an update that may permit two-player parties to enter dungeons with NPC allies. Details in Berlin.

Public Fields

More dynamic, less burden

"Improving Public Fields": more dynamic events in overworld zones, plus quality-of-life passes on questing. The intent — overworld content that meaningfully competes with instanced content for player attention.

Customization & Platforms

Animation rewards · Switch 2 · Free Trial expansion

New rewards in 8.x will let players customize character animations and actions — the closest the game has come to per-character gait/idle/cast personality. CEO Takashi Kiryu also closed the keynote by announcing a Nintendo Switch 2 version of FFXIV. Concurrently, on April 28, the Free Trial expands to include Shadowbringers — meaning new players get all content through Patch 5.58 free.

One sober note: the team estimates PS4 will be supported until roughly Patch 8.3, after which support will end.
